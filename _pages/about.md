I am a BSc in Computer Science student at Nazarbayev University (2022–June 2026) in Astana, Kazakhstan. My research focuses on Trustworthy Multilingual NLP, Multimodal Safety, Factuality, and Low-Resource Language Technologies — with a particular interest in evaluating model alignment, automating fact-checking pipelines, and studying harmful content in non-English contexts.

I am currently collaborating with Prof. Preslav Nakov at MBZUAI, where I co-developed **CHARMemes**, a 23,025-example benchmark for harmful meme detection in the Runet ecosystem, and built **Media Profiler**, a LangGraph multi-agent system for automated media bias and fact-checking. In parallel, I work as a Machine Learning Researcher at Outpeer.AI, fine-tuning Vision-Language Models for structural data extraction. I am also a Research Assistant at Nazarbayev University, where I developed a multi-agent LLM pipeline for groundwater governance analysis across 157 national laws — contributing to a manuscript in preparation for Nature Sustainability.

I am a Fellow of the Yessenov Foundation Advanced LLM Program (~5% acceptance), a Fellow of the AI Safety & LLM Evaluation Program at Monoid, and placed in the top 9 globally in the SemEval 2026 Machine-Generated Code Detection task.

I am actively seeking MSc / research opportunities in NLP, AI Safety, and Machine Learning.

## Research Interests
* Trustworthy Multilingual NLP and Factuality
* Multimodal Safety and Harmful Content Detection
* Mechanistic Interpretability and LLM Reasoning
* RAG Pipelines and Multi-Agent Systems
